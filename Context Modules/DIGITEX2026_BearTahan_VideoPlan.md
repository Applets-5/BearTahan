### Step 2 — Rehearse the prototype flow (10 mins)

**5 screens only.** This is the whole demo. Do not add more.

1. Home screen — 5 subjects, star balance, Aiden's profile visible
2. BM → Chapter 1 → one question answered correctly → mascot cheers → completion screen with stars
3. Child reward screen — tap "Claim" on Ice Cream Trip → pending state shown
4. Parent dashboard — Aiden's progress stats + chapter insights card visible
5. Parent approve screen — tap Approve → celebration

Each screen gets 2–3 seconds on screen. The voiceover drives the pace — let the screen follow the words, not the other way around.

**Do not show:** stroke tracing, Bear's Den session, BearAI chat, quests, outfit selection. These are real features but they distract from the core message in 60 seconds.

---

### Step 3 — Record the screen (10–15 mins)

- Open the Lovable prototype in Chrome, full screen
- Use **Win + G** (Windows) or **Cmd + Shift + 5** (Mac) to screen record
- **Record with no audio** — voiceover is a separate recording
- Go through the flow from Step 2 slowly and clearly — pause 1–2 seconds on each key screen
- Do it **twice** and give the editor both takes so they can pick the better one
- Save files as: `screen_take1.mp4` and `screen_take2.mp4`

---

### Step 4 — Record the voiceover (15 mins)

- Use your phone's voice memo app, or your laptop mic with any free recorder
- Speak the script from Step 1 clearly and at a steady pace
- Time yourself — **target 52–56 seconds**. If you hit 58+ seconds, you are speaking too slowly or adding words. Trim back to the script exactly.
- Record **3 takes** — the editor will pick the best one or stitch the best parts together
- Save files as: `voice_take1.m4a`, `voice_take2.m4a`, `voice_take3.m4a`

**Tips for a good take:**
- Find a quiet room — no fan noise, no traffic
- Stand up while speaking — it makes your voice sound more energetic
- Smile slightly — it comes through in your tone
- Don't rush the tagline at the end — let it land

## PART 2 — EDITOR'S JOB (while Samy is away)

**Deadline to finish and send Samy the final file: 7 May evening**
**Tool: CapCut (free) — PC or phone, both work**

---

### What you're making

A clean, professional 60-second video. No fancy transitions, no heavy effects. The goal is clear and structured, not cinematic.

---

### Step 1 — Pick the best takes

Watch both screen recordings and all 3 voiceover takes. Pick:
- The screen take where the flow is smoothest and no dead air or fumbles
- The voiceover take with the most natural, confident delivery

If no single voiceover take is perfect end-to-end, note which lines are best in which take — you can cut between them.

---

### Step 2 — Build the timeline in CapCut

**Structure to follow:**

| Timeline | What's on screen | What's being said |
|---|---|---|
| 0–3s | Team name card / static title screen | Team intro |
| 3–10s | Home screen — subjects, star balance, Aiden's profile | Hook |
| 10–20s | BM question → correct answer → mascot cheers | Core loop: lessons + stars |
| 20–28s | Completion screen with stars → child reward screen → claim pending | Reward setup lines |
| 28–38s | Parent dashboard — progress stats + Chapter Insights card (Strong/Needs Work badges visible) | AI layer line |
| 38–52s | Parent approve screen → celebration | Community/country contribution |
| 52–60s | Static end card: BearTahan logo + tagline | Tagline |

The voiceover drives all timing. Cut screen footage to fit the voice — never the reverse.

**Editor note:** The demo is intentionally simple. Resist the urge to add more screens. Five screens in 30 seconds is the right pace. The app's depth shows through what the judge hears, not how many features flash by.

---

### Step 3 — Add text overlays (keep it minimal)

Add these 3 text elements only:

1. **Opening (first 3 seconds):** `APPLE-5 | Universiti Teknologi Malaysia` — small, bottom left, white text
2. **Closing card (last 6 seconds):** `BearTahan` large, centred — and `Learn Like You Play.` smaller underneath. Plain background colour (dark blue or black). No app screen behind it.
3. **Optional mid-video:** A small label `Parent Dashboard` when the parent screen appears — helps judges understand the context instantly

---

### Step 4 — Audio check

- Voiceover should be clearly audible throughout
- No background music needed — silence is fine
- If you want subtle background music, use a CapCut royalty-free track at very low volume (20% max) so it doesn't compete with the voice
