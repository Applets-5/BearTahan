## Netizen Choice Award — Poster Strategy

The Netizen Choice Award is a **public vote**, separate from the judges' scoring. The poster will be shared publicly (social media / DIGITEX website) and must appeal to a general audience who may know nothing about education apps or KSSR.

**What the poster must achieve in 3 seconds:**
- Communicate what BearTahan is at a glance
- Look visually appealing enough to earn a vote
- Be shareable on mobile screens

**Content priorities:**
- App name: **BearTahan** — large and prominent
- One-line hook a non-technical person immediately understands
  (e.g. "Turn your child's screen time into study time" or "Learn Like You Play")
- A strong visual — app screenshot, bear mascot, or child-and-phone illustration
- Team name and UTM
- Optional: QR code linking to the video

**Keep minimal:**
- Feature lists, bullet points, technical terms (KSSR, Firebase, Flutter)
- Long descriptions — netizens scroll, they don't read

## Grand Finale — What to Prepare

The Grand Finale is a **live pitching session** on the UTM campus. Unlike the video (pre-recorded), this is face-to-face with judges and an audience.

### Demo device
- Run the live demo on a **real Android device** — not an emulator
- Use the pre-seeded Lovable prototype for the demo if the real Flutter app is not yet stable
- Pre-seed all demo data before arriving (child profile, completed chapters, Bear's Den unlocked, rewards set, BearAI pre-loaded)
- Test the device on the venue's WiFi conditions — app is online-only

### Pitch structure (suggested — confirm duration with committee)
- **Problem** (15s): Malaysian Standard 1 children on screens, not textbooks
- **Product** (30s): What BearTahan is — the core loop, live demo of lesson → stars → reward → parent approves
- **AI features** (20s): Bear's Den, BearAI — show briefly, name the benefit
- **Impact** (15s): KSSR alignment, national education agenda, scalable to any Android family
- **Close** (10s): Tagline + team

### What judges will score (from official rubric)
| Criteria | Sub-items |
|---|---|
| Functionality & Technical Implementation | Core features work, AI Integration, Technical complexity |
| Innovation & Creativity | Originality, Creative use of tech, Wow Factor |
| Impact & Problem Relevance | Problem clarity, Relevance to National Agenda, Real-world potential |
| Presentation | Design, UX, Accessibility & Responsiveness |
| Pitch / Video Submission | Clarity of explanation, Demo quality, Team presentation |

### Key things to say for each criterion
- **AI Integration:** Name all three — stroke tracing validation, Bear's Den adaptive weighting, BearAI parent consultation via Gemini API
- **National Agenda:** Say "KSSR Standard 1" and "Malaysia's national education agenda" explicitly
- **Wow Factor:** Show the mascot reacting, show the parent reward approval loop — these are the moments that land
- **Originality:** "No competitor combines daily gamified learning with a parent real-world reward system and AI-driven gap targeting in one KSSR-aligned app"
